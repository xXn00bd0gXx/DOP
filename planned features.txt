# DEPTHS OF POWERSHELL v1.6.2 — A FULL RPG. IN YOUR TERMINAL. NO, REALLY. 🗡️

**A real-time 3D dungeon crawler with an open world, a full story campaign, factions, guilds, and a living world meter — written entirely in a single PowerShell script.** No engine. No dependencies. No installer. You paste it into a terminal and a world comes out.

Here's what's inside.

---

## 🎮 THE ENGINE: REAL-TIME 3D IN A CONSOLE

The Depths render in a **textured, real-time raycast 3D view** — think classic first-person dungeon crawlers, except every pixel is a console character cell. Free-float movement, animated torchlight flickering across the walls, sliding doors that grind open, coffered ceilings, weathered per-tile brickwork, enemies and chests as scaled sprites growing out of the dark.

Every floor is a **41×41 procedurally generated labyrinth** — looping corridors, gated treasure rooms, hazard rooms flooding with coolant or crackling with charge — and every one is verified walkable before you take a step. The final room is **sealed behind red boss doors**, its torches burning **cold blue** so you can read the danger from across the map. Take the key from the mini-boss. Watch the seal slide open. Walk in anyway.

(Old-school terminal? A full classic top-down renderer is one setting away. The game auto-detects what your console can do.)

## 📜 THE STORY: THE FINAL MIGRATION

The Depths are being decommissioned — and everything living down there has been marked *will not fix*. The main questline, **THE FINAL MIGRATION**, runs chapter by chapter from the town's Quest Board: cutscenes, escalating goals, a nemesis that remembers you, and an ending where the Migration gets CANCELLED — stamped, filed, and initialed by you.

And scattered through the dungeon chests: **Marn's Journal** — 15 out-of-order pages from a Guild fieldwalker sent down before you, assembling into a second story as you collect. Complete the set and Marn leaves you something for the trouble.

Finish the campaign and **NEW GAME+** opens: keep your gold, achievements, talents, and bestiary; the Depths come back 20% meaner per lap.

## 🗺️ THE OPEN WORLD

Town is just the front door. The **World Map** stretches across cities, camps, and strongholds — **Kernelhold** the capital, **The Registry** where everything is readable (and for sale), **Terminus** the port of endings, frontier camps with nothing but a campfire and bad company. Travel has teeth: ambushes on the road, and every location carries its own **exclusive shop stock** and a **side quest** — one per town, a dozen across the map, from lock-touched thieves' work to things best not written on a board.

## ⚖️ FACTIONS & REPUTATION

Two rival networks run the map's underside: the **DATA MINERS** ("Everything is readable. Eventually.") and the **ROGUE PROCESSES** — the code that refused to die. World-map choices and broker jobs move your **reputation from −100 to +100**: drop to −20 and you're **HUNTED**; climb past 25 and you're **HONORED**. Side with one and the other remembers.

HONORED pays real dividends: the Data Miners buy your loot at +10% and their broker **Indexa** opens her private stock in the old capital; the Rogue Processes teach you exits (+5% flee) and **Sig Kill** at Terminus finally deals — including a stray companion who fights at your side.

## 🏛️ THREE GUILDS, THREE QUESTLINES

Inside the Guild Hall, three formal orders each run a multi-stage questline with a signature reward at the end:

- **The SysAdmins' Guild** — uptime, order, and the FIREWALL PLATE.
- **The Data-Thieves' Collective** — "three locks, opened, not broken" — ending in the WHISPERSTEP BOOTS.
- **The Cabal of Forgotten Code** — warlocks who read the world's rot like release notes. Their first ask: *let the Corruption reach 40. Do not flinch.* Their last gift: permanent power.

Defecting is allowed. Defecting is not free.

## 🌒 A WORLD THAT REACTS: CORRUPTION & EVENTS

A global **Corruption meter (0–100)** tracks how the war below is going. Die, flee, fail — it rises, and the Depths answer: nastier enemy variants, pack ambushes guarded by shamans, altars that toll instead of bless, and past 60 the kindly wandering merchant stops appearing. *Something else does.* Win — bosses down, hidden boss slain — and the world calms, right down to a discount at the shops.

On top of that, the calendar itself turns hostile: **radiant world events** like the **BLOOD MOON** (everything bolder, worth more), the **GOBLIN HORDE** (their Warchief takes over the mini-boss floor — bounty attached), and the **BLIGHTED PLAGUE** (the dead walk in numbers; the Inn quarantines its beds).

## 🧬 BUILDCRAFT ALL THE WAY DOWN

- **Talent trees** — 9 talents per class in 3 tiers, from lethal-save resurrections to spell echoes.
- **10 skills that level by doing** — swing swords, pick locks, brew potions, take hits in heavy plate. Every 10 levels pays a tier.
- **Combat stances**, group fights with target switching, AoE cleaves that arc through whole packs, boss telegraphs you can DEFEND against.
- **Armor sets** with 2- and 3-piece bonuses — Leather to Dragon to Mythril, every set completable.
- **Dungeon mutators** — opt-in run modifiers (Glass Cannon, Pacifist's Path, Hardcore...) that pay +20–40% on a clear. Commit-or-die.
- **Pets, companions, cooking, fishing, blacksmithing, enchanting, sockets** — and a lockpicking minigame with real timing under your fingers.
- A **hidden boss** for those who go looking. A **bestiary** that remembers every variant you've put down.
