- COMBAT OVERALL ITEMS:

Stance system — Let the player switch between stances (Aggressive / Balanced / Defensive) that shift stats. Aggressive = more damage, less defense. Gives a persistent tactical layer.
Enemy telegraph system — Display messages like "The Ogre raises its club high..." to warn of a big attack next turn. The player then has to decide: defend, use a stun, burn it down, or heal up. This is your charge mechanic extended to more variety.
Encounter Variety
Multi-enemy fights — Target selection becomes a decision. Do you focus the healer? AoE the swarm? CC the heavy hitter?
Enemy synergies — A shaman that buffs other enemies, or a pair of wolves that get a "Pack Tactics" bonus when both are alive. Forces prioritization.
Boss phases — Bosses change behavior at health thresholds (new attacks, enrage, summon adds, heal). Keeps long fights from feeling repetitive.
Environmental hazards — Traps or terrain effects in certain fights. "The room is flooding — combat ends in 5 turns or you drown." Adds urgency.
Progression & Player Expression
Skill tree or ability unlocks — Let players choose which abilities they learn so different runs feel different.

Rendering upgrade (DOP Final edition):

Replace Clear-Host with cursor repositioning — eliminates the blank-frame flash
Single-write output via StringBuilder + [Console]::Write() or SetBufferContents
Move raycasting math to inline C# — this is where you'll reclaim the most CPU time
Dirty rectangles — only worthwhile once the other optimizations are in place`

Pass enemy/sprite data into the C# method as arrays if you want to render billboarded sprites (classic Doom-style).
Return more than just chars — you could return an int[] of color codes alongside the char buffer, then apply ANSI colors on the PowerShell side or handle it all in C# with a full ANSI string builder.
Recompilation is free to retry — if you change the C# code, you need to restart the PowerShell session since Add-Type can't redefine a type. During development, test in short-lived sessions or use a helper script.
Use a proper DDA algorithm (stepping by grid lines rather than fixed small steps) if you want more precision and speed. The example above uses a simple fixed-step march for clarity.
